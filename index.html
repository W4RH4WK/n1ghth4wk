Preface

Vanilla Shadowrun™ features a complicated rule set and while I don't know whether the game is even sufficiently balanced, I doubt this amount of complexity is needed to give the game enough depth to be enjoyable. Shadowrun 6th Edition was supposed to simplify the rules, but was rushed and arrived broken beyond repair in my opinion. On my search for a simpler version, I discovered Shadowrun Anarchy. Shadowrun Anarchy is based on 5th Edition and draws a lot of parallels while actually establishing its own, far simpler, rule set. This is the starting point for my approach.

This document states the homebrew rules that will govern my play sessions. These rules are mostly based on Shadowrun Anarchy and will be extended over time. As always, the GM retains the right to overrule established rules. There is neither fluff nor lore here, just mechanics; everything else is found in the original books.

Turns and Narrations

Each Turn, every player has a chance to play out and describe their character's actions, along with other elements of the story going on around them. These descriptions are called Narrations, and as the game progresses these Narrations build on each other and form the story of the game. [SRA]

The Turn Order is situation dependent and may be adjusted by spending Plot Points. If push comes to shove, compare characters A + E to determine who goes first.

Tests

6-sided dice are used to determine the outcome of certain actions. Tests are typically opposed rolls, the attacker and defender roll dice simultaneously and compare Hits. The defender sets the threshold for the attacker. To speed things up, the GM may use a fixed threshold instead of a defender's roll.

Repeating Tests

Most Tests can be repeated for an accumulative -2 modifier.

Extended Tests

Some Tests take far longer than a moment and therefore require the character to invest time. An Extended Test accumulates Hits over multiple rolls to meet a given (high) threshold. Each roll beyond the first suffers the regular -2 modifier of Repeating Tests. A fixed time interval is spent along with each roll.

Teamwork Tests

Outside combat, characters can work together to overcame various obstacles. A Teamwork Test is done by selecting a leader who will do the majority of the work. Other characters assist by rolling a corresponding Test. Each helper's Hit adds a bonus die to the leader's dice pool. The number of bonus dice is limited by the leader's Skill (or higher Attribute).

Glitch Die

A Plot Point can be spent to introduce the Glitch Die. The Glitch Die is rolled separate from the regular dice pool and does not count towards the Hits. The effect of the Glitch Die is an addition to the regular Tests's outcome.

Rounding

Plot Points

Plot Points are tokens that can be redeemed for special effects. Both, players and the GM, have the ability to spend a Plot Point. When a Plot Point is spent, the spender hands the token to the opposition.

  • Players begin the game with 3 Plot Points each and may be awarded more points by the GM
  • Players may have a maximum of 5 Plot Points
  • Players can only spend one Plot Point at a time
  • The GM starts with 1 Plot Point

Qualities

Qualities are additional traits of your character that come in positive and negative forms. Characters typically start out with 2 positive and 1 negative Quality.

Amps

Every more-human-than-human improvement to your character is handled as an Amp. Ranging from cybernetic implants (Cyberware) to Spells, to Cyberdecks, etc.

Combat

Actions

The GM determines whether an action is either a Simple Action, a Complex Action, or a Bonus Action. A character can take either 1 Complex Action or 2 Simple Actions, in addition to their Bonus Action. A general limit of one attack action per Turn is imposed. Though, certain Amps allow you to exceed this limit.

Attack & Defense

A + Skill vs. A + L

The type of attack determines the Skill used by the attacker.

Net Hits typically result in addition points of damage.

Unarmed Combat inflicts S / 2 Stun damage.

Hacking

L + Hacking vs. L + Firewall

  • VR grants a +2 modifier to all related Tests
  • Stun damage always applies to the Cyberdeck's Condition Monitor
  • Physical damage is applied to the decker when in VR (armor is ignored), to the Cyberdeck otherwise

Technomancers

  • Use L + L to for Matrix Defense
  • Sprites can be summoned with Hacking
  • Only 1 Sprite can be summoned at the same time
  • Technomancers deal L points of damage when attacking a Sprite directly.

Vehicle / Drone Combat

  • Vehicles / drones use A + L of the driver to defend
  • VR grants a +2 modifier to all related Tests

Spells

  • Attack Spells typically state how to defend
  • Only 1 Spell can be sustained at a time
  • Spells featuring Armor Avoidance (AA) make Net Hits count as Armor Penetration instead of bonus damage
  • Damage from Astral Combat is applied as Stun damage

Spirits

  • Only 1 spirit can be conjured at a time
  • Spirits ignore conventional weapons
  • Spirits can be damaged with W + Close Combat -2. Unarmed damage is applied.
