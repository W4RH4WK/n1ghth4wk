Shadowrun Alex

Preface

Shadowrun originally features a rather complicated rule set and while I don't know whether the game is actually sufficiently balanced to begin with, I think this amount of complexity might not be necessary to give the game enough depth to be enjoyable. Shadowrun 6th Edition was supposed to simplify a lot of the rules and streamline existing systems. However, 6th Edition was rushed and is now broken beyond repair in my opinion. On my search for a simpler version of Shadowrun, I discovered Shadowrun Anarchy, which is referred to as the rules-lite version of Shadowrun. Shadowrun Anarchy is based on 5th Edition and draws a lot of parallels while actually establishing its own rule set. This is the starting point for my approach.

This document establishes the basic set of homebrew rules that will govern my play sessions. These rules are mostly based on Shadowrun Anarchy, but will be extended over time. As always, the GM retains the right to overrule the established rules. There is no fluff and no lore here, just mechanics; everything else is found in the original books. Descriptions are kept concise and sometimes incomplete.

Turns and Narrations

Each turn, every player has a chance to play out and describe their character's actions, along with other elements of the story going on around them. These descriptions are called Narrations, and as the game progresses these Narrations build on each other and form the story of the game. [SR:A]

The order is situation dependent and may be adjusted by spending Plot Points. If push comes to shove, characters A + E can be compared to determine who goes first.

Checks

6-sided dice are used to determine the outcome of certain actions. Checks are typically opposed rolls, the attacker and defender roll dice simultaneously and compare. The defender sets the threshold for the attacker. To speed things up, the GM may use a fixed threshold instead of a defender's roll.

Repeating Checks

Most checks can be repeated for an accumulative -2 dice modifier.

Extended Checks

Some checks take far longer than a moment and therefore require the character to invest time. An extended check accumulates hits over multiple rolls to meet a given (high) threshold. Each roll beyond the first suffers the regular -2 dice modifier of repeating checks. A fixed time interval is spent along with each roll.

Teamwork Checks

Outside combat, characters can work together to overcame various obstacles. A Teamwork Check is done by selecting a leader who will do the majority of the work. Other characters my assist by rolling a corresponding check. Each helper's hit adds a bonus die to the leader's dice pool. The number of bonus dice is limited by the leader's Skill (or higher Attribute).

Glitch Die

A Plot Point can be spent to introduce the Glitch Die. The Glitch Die is rolled separate from the regular dice pool and does not count towards the hits.

Plot Points

Plot Points are tokens that can be redeemed for special effects. Both, players and the GM, have the ability to spend a Plot Point. When a Plot Point is spent, the spender gives it to the opposition.

Players begin the game with 3 Plot Points each and may be awarded more points by the GM. Players may have a maximum of 5 Plot Points. Players can only spend one Plot Point at a time. The GM starts with 1 Plot Point.
